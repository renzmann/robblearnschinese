Robb Learns Chinese

Journaling my road from 0 to fluent in Mandarin.

What's this site?

After putting roughly two years and roughly two thousand hours into learning Japanese, I'd like to give Mandarin a try (shocking, I know). While there's plenty of stories, opinions, and advice for people who went for a primarily input-based approach to Japanese, I can't say the same for Mandarin. Moreover, I took a hybrid approach with Japanese; with daily bilingual Anki use and the occasional textbook for reference and grammar points.

For Japanese, my primary motivation was simply a better understanding of media I consume on a daily basis, and if possible, consuming it in its native language. I don't have any native speakers at my disposal aside from iTalki, and while trips to Japan are nice, they aren't a luxury I can do regularly enough to say that fluency in speaking would give me a good return on the time invested. If I'm able to keep consuming books, games, and TV in Japanese, then I've pretty much hit my goal with it.

Chinese is a different beast, though. I have at least eighteen native speakers for coworkers, representing many different regions of China. I want to speak their language with them, even if it's just a limited subset of a standard Mandarin dialect. Moreover, I want a better appreciation for the varied cultures they represent. So with that in mind, I'm planning to take a completely pure ALG approach to Chinese.

This website will serve two purposes for me:

  1. Learn to write clean vanilla HTML/CSS/JS
  2. Catalog my experience each week with Chinese through the seven ALG (Dreaming Spanish style) levels

I regret not writing about my comprehension levels as I traveled through the stages of acquiring Japanese. Of course I remember there was a "complete gibberish" stage of Japanese, but it's not really until I was getting help planning a trip to Kyoto with a native speaker in Japanese that I thought to reflect on my progress. So I'll be using this site to keep myself accountable and as a living record for a 100% self-study ALG approach.
