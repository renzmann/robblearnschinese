date: 2026-5-11 hours: 1619 title: 1600+ hour update ---

I didn't think I'd notice a difference between the ~1400 mark from a couple months ago and now, but I'm glad to be mistaken. That 1400 was just a best guess, so this '1619' is also just an estimate, but still represents 219 hours of progress since then. I'm not exactly sure what changed, but I know there's been a big improvement in my listening comprehension since then.

I've completely dropped Anki and reading without audio for the time being. I don't know if this change specifically made a difference, but I'm certain my listening comprehension has solidified since then. Almost like turning around a bend in difficulty, I have a solid enough base for most content I come across, and the words I don't know are starting to ring clear in my ear. 200 hours ago, if there was a word I was completely unfamiliar with, it was common for it to just completely fly by, as if just rubbed out with an eraser. Now, as long as I have the rest of the sentence in my head, I can hear the sounds of an unknown word clearly.

Unfortunately, most native domains are still too hard. While I can somewhat follow the news with visual cues, the language they use is still totally foreign. I also recently ran into an out-of-context Naruto clip and while I was sure it was Japanese, I hadn't a clue what he said.

YUYU's podcast (currently around the 430 ep. mark) is hard enough to feel challenging while still comprehensible. It varies a lot in difficulty, and can go anywhere between 50% for sections where he quotes anime or books, and 99% comprehension when he's talking about generic stuff like "why I like going to the sauna." Teppei beginner is now 100% comprehension without fail. Bite Size Japanese is a bit easier than YUYU, but above Teppei, and fairly consistent around 98%-ish comprehension. CIJ is fantastic as always, and everything in the 85-95 difficulty score range is probably perfect for my level. The Botchan videos are still a bit too hard.

As for native content I can understand, I'm really enjoying my second watch through of Cardcaptor Sakura. While it was comprehensible through visual cues before, it's like the language had a gauze lifted off of it this time around. Through my first watch I found Shaoran difficult to understand, Kero completely unintelligible, and Touya also basically incomprehensible. Kero is still hard, but I can usually catch the gist. Sometimes I miss dialogue from Touya, but my familiarity with the content can guide me through it. Shaoran is very easy to understand now. I still run into a lot of new words, though. 神主 is one that came up recently that I didn't recognize while listening, but of course it's meaning was obvious through context.

I've also started watching the original Sailor Moon from 1992. Far better than I expected; this show is a blast. It's just silly fun, and much like Cardcaptor, the monster-of-the-week format is perfect for acquiring new language. I had trouble with 水晶 the first few times it came up, but definitely can't say the same now. When the villains are talking to each other it gets extremely difficult to understand, maybe even less than 10% comprehension, but usually this doesn't hinder my overall understanding of the episode or overarching story.
